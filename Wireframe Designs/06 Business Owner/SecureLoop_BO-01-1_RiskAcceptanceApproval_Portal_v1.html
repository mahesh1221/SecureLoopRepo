A business risk needs your decision

High Risk Customer Payment Processing Requested by Priya Menon · Control Owner

Payment gateway is running on software the vendor no longer patches.

Keeping it live while we migrate means customers can keep paying — but an attacker finding a new flaw in this software would face fewer defences than on a current version.

CISO Business Impact Summary
What this means for the business

What's wrong. The software powering our checkout runs a version the vendor stopped supporting last quarter. There are no new security fixes available for it.

Why it's still running. Finance and Ops need the quarterly close cycle to finish before we can swap this component without freezing card processing. The replacement is already in staging; go-live window is week 37.

What could happen. If an attacker discovers a new flaw in this software before we migrate, customer card data in transit could be exposed. Best estimate of fallout: £1.8M – £2.4M (customer notification, regulator fine, short-term gateway swap). Card processing would pause for roughly 18–36 hours during response.

What we've done to reduce the odds. Gateway is now behind a WAF tuned to block known exploits for this version, outbound egress is restricted, and the security team has a watch rule live 24/7.

If you approve, this risk is accepted for 90 days — until 20 Jul 2026.
After that, it auto-escalates to the Security Committee unless remediated or extended.
Acceptance details
Control Owner's justification

Quarterly close cycle blocks any planned downtime on the payments path until week 37. Replacement component is built, tested in staging, and holding a firm go-live date. Early migration forfeits full reconciliation of Q2.

Compensating controls in place
  • WAF rule tuned to block known exploits
  • Outbound egress restricted from gateway
  • 24/7 SOC watch-rule on gateway traffic
  • Replacement component signed off in staging
