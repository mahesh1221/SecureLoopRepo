FND-2026-1092 · Request submitted Jan 17, 2026

Deprecated TLS cipher on customer-facing load balancer

High LB-01-customer-edge Owner: Sarah Chen
Pending CISO Review

What is the risk?

One of our customer-facing load balancers is still configured to support an older encryption method (known as TLS 1.1) alongside modern ones. While most current customer browsers and apps use the newer, more secure method, a small minority of legacy clients still rely on the older one.

Why does it exist?

Approximately 0.3% of our active customer base — primarily international customers using older browsers or mobile operating systems — cannot connect to our platform without this older encryption method. Disabling it would immediately cut off these users.

What could happen if exploited?

An attacker with network-level access (e.g., on a shared Wi-Fi or a compromised ISP path) could theoretically intercept traffic from legacy clients and decrypt sensitive session data. This risk does not affect customers using modern browsers.

What would the business impact be?

If exploited against our customer segment using legacy clients:

Financial: Estimated $180,000 - $350,000 in regulatory penalties and customer notification costs if a breach occurred. Operational: Potential reputational impact with affected customer segment; limited to ~12,000 of our 4.1M active users. Reputational: If disclosed publicly, moderate impact; we would cite the trade-off with legacy client support.

Recommendation

Accept this risk for 90 days while the engineering team builds a fallback experience for legacy clients (projected Q2). After that, deprecate TLS 1.1 entirely.
